### Powering Bike On

1. * Disengage the kickstand, grab the brake handle to engage the brakes, and seat yourself on the Super73.
   * Press the battery power button to turn the bike on.
   * Once the display screen is illuminated, the throttle or pedal assist is active and you’re ready to ride.

### Changing the information viewed on the display

Quick press “O” button to toggle the information viewed on the display.

1. * Speed (mph)
   * Range (miles)
   * Assist (0-4)
   * Total (miles)

### Operating Lights

The Super73 RX model comes equipped with a front headlight and rear brake light.

1. * To activate the front headlight, press and hold down the UP “^” arrow button on the display until the light illuminates. Follow the same step to turn off the lights.
   * The rear brake light will automatically be illuminated when the bike is powered on. The rear light will get brighter when the brake levers are engaged.
   * You can also activate the lights via the App

### Pedal Assist

To view your pedal assist mode, follow these steps once the display has been turned on and you are seated on the Super73:

* Press on the menu “O” button briefly to switch between display views until “Assist” appears on the screen.
* Press the up “^” button to increase the pedal-assist level. To decrease the assist level, press the down “v” button. Pedal assist ranges from 1-4, with 0 meaning pedal assist is turned off. The higher the level, the more electric assistance you’ll get, and the less you’ll have to pedal.

  + Level 1 (ECO) - 25% Speed Power
  + Level 2 (TOUR) - 50% Speed Power
  + Level 3 (SPORT) - 75% Speed Power
  + Level 4 (SUPER) - 100% Speed Power
* Once you begin pedaling, the pedal assist sensor will activate the motor and you will begin to accelerate to the selected assist level.
* The display will revert to previous viewing mode after desired assist level is chosen.

### Settings Menu

Hold down the “O” button on display to activate the settings menu.

Settings parameters:

* Contrast (display window contrast 0-100)
* Units (standard is set to mi/F)
* Position (changes the orientation of display if it is on the left or right side of the bike)
* Backlight (display backlight power 0-100)
* Version is the software version of the system.
* Coin is the battery level inside display.
